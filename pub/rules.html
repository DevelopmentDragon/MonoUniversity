Mono University

Rules
How to Win
Move around the board buying as many properties (Buildings, TTC Stations and Libraries (CL and RL)) as you can. You can start buying only once you've crossed GO. Each player starts with $1,500. The more you own, the more rent you'll get. If you're the last player when all other players have gone bankrupt, you win!

On Your Turn
  • Roll the dice.
  • Your token will move forward that number of spaces.
  • Where did you land?
    • If you landed on a Building, you'll have the chance to buy that property (if unowned) or you'll have to pay rent to the owner!
    • If you landed on an action space (see the section below), you'll have to take the relevant action.
  • If you rolled a double, you can roll the dice again and take another turn.
  • Watch out! If you roll a double 3 times in the same turn, you must
    GO TO JAIL!
  • After moving, you can manage your property, if you want to. You can either buy a building or build on it. You can only initiate these actions on your turn.

Board Spaces
  • Buildings: Click on any building tile to view its information on the bottom left at any time.
  • Unowned Buildings: When you land on a Building that no one owns and you have crossed GO, you can either buy the Building within the time limit or do nothing until your turn is up.
  • Owned Buildings: When you land on a Building that someone else owns, you must pay them rent!
Tokens
The different colours of the tokens on the board are immaterial and just used to differentiate the players.
  • Player: Default piece, no special abilities
  • Cop: Only get two turns in Jail for being sent by a tile, four if by Chance or Community Chest cards
  • Lawyer: Earn three times the Get Out of Jail cards. All expenses increased by 10%.
  • Veteran: Reduction on money spent on normal tiles by 10%, but collect only $150 when crossing GO.
  • Worker: Get $250 by passing GO or $500 by landing on it. By cards and tax tiles, add $25 and $50 respectively.
  • Genius: Unaffected by Utility and TTC tiles. Pay 25% more on tax tiles.
  • Banker: Immune to Tax Tiles, but rents are 5% more expensive.
  • Investor: Buy properties early but they cost 50% more early game.
